Social Determinants of Substance Misuse

A Data Science Approach to Identifying Socio-economic Predictors of Substance Misuse in England

Introduction

This project analyses socio-economic factors contributing to substance misuse, employing data science techniques to identify key indicators for targeted intervention strategies.

Data Sources

  • Public Health England (PHE)
  • Office for National Statistics (ONS)
  • NHS Digital

Methodology

Data was collected and processed using no-code/low-code tools like Power BI, Excel, and KNIME. Analytical techniques include regression analysis, clustering, and data visualization.

Key Findings

The analysis revealed significant socio-economic predictors of substance misuse, including income levels, unemployment rates, and educational attainment.

Recommendations

Advanced predictive modelling and integration of additional datasets can further enhance the accuracy of identifying high-risk regions for targeted interventions.

Social Determinants of Substance Misuse

A Data Science Approach to Identifying Socio-economic Predictors of Substance Misuse in England

Introduction

This project explores how socio-economic factors such as income, employment, education, and housing contribute to substance misuse across different regions in England. By leveraging data science techniques, it identifies key socio-economic indicators and proposes targeted intervention strategies to mitigate substance misuse.

Project Overview

The project "Social Determinants of Substance Misuse â€“ A Data Science Approach" is a comprehensive data-driven analysis aimed at understanding the socio-economic predictors of substance misuse in economically disadvantaged regions of England. The analysis employs data science methodologies to identify patterns, assess risk levels, and recommend actionable interventions for policymakers and healthcare providers.

The data analysis was conducted using datasets from Public Health England (PHE), the Office for National Statistics (ONS), and NHS Digital. The focus was on key socio-economic indicators such as income, unemployment, education, and housing metrics. Advanced data processing, regression analysis, clustering, and data visualisation techniques were utilised to uncover critical insights into substance misuse trends.
